Washington, DC deadlines and verification

DC benchmarking deadlines and third-party verification

The current annual benchmarking deadline for covered private buildings in Washington, DC is May 1. The next third-party verification deadline is May 1, 2027 for calendar year 2026 data, and the requirement then repeats every six years.

What changed

Current law no longer matches older deadline snippets

Current District guidance changed the benchmarking and verification deadline from April 1 to May 1 and changed verification from every three years to every six years.

Some older pages in the ecosystem still show the old timing, so reporting teams should align their working calendar to the current BEAM guidance instead of recycled snippets.

Coverage

Which buildings are covered now

Current District guidance says private buildings over 10,000 square feet are covered. If a building is covered, the reporting process needs a clean annual record whether or not the team is also planning around future verification or BEPS cycles.

Verification

What third-party verification means

Third-party verification is a separate quality check on the benchmark and related Portfolio Manager statements. It does not replace annual reporting. It changes how early teams need to organize source data and assumptions.

Prep timeline

A practical 90 / 30 / 7 day checklist

90 days before filing

  • Confirm the covered building list and who owns reporting responsibility.
  • Check utility account coverage and missing periods.
  • Start resolving building record inconsistencies.

30 days before filing

  • Run QA on anomalies, missing values, and implausible intensities.
  • Document assumptions and unresolved issues.
  • Prepare the handoff into Portfolio Manager.

7 days before filing

  • Finalize review.
  • Confirm submission readiness.
  • Make sure the audit trail is complete enough for later questions.
Common submission failures

Where reporting cycles usually break down

  • Late discovery of missing utility periods.
  • Inconsistent building metadata across teams.
  • Weak documentation for unusual values.
  • Overreliance on manual last-minute reconciliation.
FAQ

Frequently asked deadline questions

Why do some DC pages still say April 1?

Older materials reflect the pre-amendment rule. Current law and current BEAM guidance use May 1.

Does my building have to benchmark if it is just over 10,000 sq. ft.?

Yes. Current District guidance says private buildings over 10,000 square feet are covered.

When do I need third-party verification?

The next deadline is May 1, 2027 for calendar year 2026 data.

What happens if my reporting data has errors?

Errors create rework, weaken defensibility, and make verification and internal review harder.

Where does Portfolio Manager fit in?

Portfolio Manager is the reporting system. The hard operational work usually happens before data is entered there.
