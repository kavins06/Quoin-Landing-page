Washington, DC energy benchmarking

Washington, DC requires private buildings over 10,000 square feet to benchmark annually and report through ENERGY STAR Portfolio Manager by May 1. Quoin is a free guided platform that helps building owners set up the building, connect Portfolio Manager, organize required data, and catch issues before reporting.

At a glance

Current District benchmarking basics

Covered private buildings Over 10,000 square feet.
Annual reporting deadline May 1.
Reporting platform ENERGY STAR Portfolio Manager.
Verification timing Next third-party verification due in 2027 for 2026 data; then every six years.
Definition

What DC energy benchmarking is

DC energy benchmarking is the annual process of tracking and reporting whole-building energy and water performance for covered buildings through ENERGY STAR Portfolio Manager.

Coverage

Who has to benchmark in Washington, DC

Current District guidance says private buildings over 10,000 square feet must benchmark annually. If you own a covered building, the priority is to get the property set up correctly, assemble complete energy data, and finish the benchmark before May 1.

Workflow

How Quoin fits the workflow

Quoin gives building owners a guided place to move through the steps that usually create delays: setting up the building, connecting Portfolio Manager, organizing utility data, resolving missing coverage, and reviewing the record before reporting.

  • Set up the building and see the required inputs in one place.
  • Connect Portfolio Manager and bring the benchmarking workflow together earlier.
  • Review missing data, unusual values, and open issues before the deadline.
Portfolio Manager

Portfolio Manager is the reporting system. Quoin helps you get there with a cleaner record.

ENERGY STAR Portfolio Manager is still the system used for DC benchmarking. Most of the work happens before you report: getting the building details right, gathering utility data, checking coverage, resolving gaps, and reviewing the benchmark with confidence.

Common failure points

Why owners get stuck

  • The property is not fully set up until too close to the deadline.
  • Utility accounts or meter coverage are incomplete.
  • Missing periods and unusual values are found too late to fix calmly.
  • Nobody has one clear view of what is done, missing, or still under review.
FAQ

Frequently asked questions

What is DC energy benchmarking?

It is the District's annual requirement for covered buildings to report energy and water performance through ENERGY STAR Portfolio Manager.

Which buildings have to benchmark in Washington, DC?

Current District guidance says private buildings over 10,000 square feet are covered.

Do I still need ENERGY STAR Portfolio Manager?

Yes. Portfolio Manager is the reporting system used for DC benchmarking. Quoin helps you get the information ready before you report.

Is Quoin free to use?

Yes. Quoin is a free public-interest platform built to help DC building owners complete benchmarking with less friction.

What do I need before I start?

You should have the building details, utility account coverage, and the energy and water data needed for reporting. If anything is missing, Quoin helps you spot it early.
