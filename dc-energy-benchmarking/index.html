Washington, DC energy benchmarking

Washington, DC requires annual energy benchmarking for private buildings over 10,000 square feet. Quoin helps owners, operators, consultants, and asset managers collect utility data, clean building records, and prepare submission-ready reports before the May 1 deadline.

Definition

What DC energy benchmarking is

DC benchmarking is the annual process of tracking and reporting whole-building energy and water performance through ENERGY STAR Portfolio Manager for covered buildings in the District.

Coverage

Who has to benchmark in Washington, DC

Current District guidance says private buildings over 10,000 square feet are covered and must benchmark annually. Reports are due each year by May 1.

How Quoin fits the workflow

Portfolio Manager

Portfolio Manager without the data scramble

Portfolio Manager is still the reporting system. The operational problem is everything that happens before submission: collecting utility data, matching it to the correct building record, resolving gaps, and documenting decisions. That is where Quoin is designed to help.

Common failure points

Why teams miss the deadline

  • Utility data is incomplete or scattered across accounts.
  • Building records are inconsistent across internal systems.
  • Anomalies are found too late to investigate calmly.
  • Portfolio Manager becomes the first place data issues are discovered.
